🔬 Role in the Muscular System
🦴
Connected System
Skeletal System

Muscles attach to bones via tendons and pull on them as levers. The two systems are so interdependent they are often called the musculoskeletal system — one is nearly useless without the other.

🧠
Connected System
Nervous System

Every voluntary movement starts as a nerve impulse. Motor neurons carry signals from the brain to the neuromuscular junction, where acetylcholine triggers muscle fibres to contract.

🫀
Connected System
Circulatory System

Working muscles need oxygen and glucose delivered fast. Blood flow can increase 20x during exercise. The circulatory system also removes lactic acid and CO2 as waste products of muscle activity.
